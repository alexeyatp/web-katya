About

Welcome to my personal website! I, Elena Vladimirovna Ivanova, am a Senior Lecturer at the Department of Information Computing Systems with more than 15 years of teaching and research experience.

My professional activities are focused on computer modeling, machine learning algorithm development, and big data analysis. In my work, I strive to combine deep theoretical knowledge with practical applications, preparing students for real challenges in the rapidly changing field of information technology.

In my teaching practice, I follow an interactive approach to learning that stimulates critical thinking and a creative approach to problem-solving. I believe that education should be not only informative but also inspiring, awakening in students the desire to independently explore and discover new things.

On this website, you will find information about my research activities, educational materials for the courses I teach, class schedules and consultation hours, as well as contact information. I look forward to collaboration and am open to discussing interesting projects and ideas.

Participation in International Conference on Artificial Intelligence

April 10, 2025

Last week, I presented a paper on "Application of Deep Learning in Time Series Forecasting" at an international conference in Astana.

The conference brought together over 200 specialists in the field of artificial intelligence from 15 countries. In my presentation, I shared the results of research conducted jointly with our department's graduate students over the past year.

Main points of the presentation:

  • Using recurrent neural networks for time series analysis
  • Comparative analysis of LSTM and GRU architectures on various datasets
  • Methods for hyperparameter optimization to improve prediction accuracy
  • Practical application of the developed models in industry

The presentation generated significant interest from the audience and sparked subsequent discussion. Particularly valuable was the discussion on regularization methods to combat overfitting of models on small datasets.

As a result of the conference, our project was recognized as one of the most promising for practical implementation. Further research in this direction is planned, taking into account the recommendations and comments received.

New Publication in "Data Science Today" Journal

April 2, 2025

I'm pleased to announce the publication of my article "Optimization of Clustering Algorithms for Big Data Analysis" in the international journal "Data Science Today".

This article is the result of two years of research, during which new methods were developed to improve the performance of clustering algorithms when working with large volumes of data.

Key achievements presented in the article:

  • Development of a modified k-means algorithm with dynamic centroid initialization
  • Creation of a parallel implementation of hierarchical clustering for multiprocessor systems
  • Introduction of data preprocessing techniques that accelerate the clustering process by 45%
  • Experimental validation of the proposed methods on real datasets

The article underwent rigorous peer review and received high marks from experts in the field. The "Data Science Today" journal has an impact factor of 3.8 and is ranked in Q1 for "Computer Science & Artificial Intelligence".

The full text of the article is available by subscription in the journal's electronic library. It will be accessible to students and graduate students of the department in the "Scientific Publications" section of the educational portal.

Successful Thesis Defense by Students

March 25, 2025

Congratulations to the students of group IS-401 for successfully defending their theses! All projects received high marks from the committee.

This year, 6 thesis projects were defended under my supervision, all receiving "excellent" grades. I would like to particularly highlight the following works:

  • Akhmetov Arman - "Development of an Intelligent Urban Traffic Monitoring System Based on Computer Vision"
  • Sergeeva Elena - "Creation of a Recommendation System for an Educational Platform Using Machine Learning"
  • Kozlov Dmitry - "Design and Implementation of a Distributed System for Medical Data Storage and Processing"

Elena Sergeeva's thesis project was noted by the committee chair as having high potential for implementation and was recommended for further development within the master's program.

All defended works were distinguished by relevant topics, thorough theoretical foundation, and quality software implementation. The students demonstrated a high level of proficiency in modern technologies and methods for developing information systems.

I wish all graduates successful professional paths and hope to see many of them as master's students in our department!

Research Projects

I am a leader and participant in several research projects at the national and international levels. Key areas of work include the development of intelligent data analysis systems and optimization of machine learning algorithms.

Current Projects:

Development of a Predictive Analytics System for Industrial Enterprises

2023-2025
Grant from the Ministry of Education and Science of Kazakhstan

This project aims to create an intelligent data analysis system for predicting emergency situations and optimizing technological processes at industrial facilities. The system being developed uses a combination of machine learning algorithms and statistical methods to process large amounts of data received from IoT devices.

Key results:

  • Developed architecture for a distributed data collection and processing system
  • Implemented algorithms for early detection of anomalies in equipment operation
  • Created a prototype recommendation system for reducing energy consumption

Development of Intelligent Data Processing Algorithms for Medical Diagnostics

2024-2026
International grant in collaboration with the University of Melbourne

This project focuses on developing machine learning algorithms for analyzing medical images and biometric data for early diagnosis of various diseases. Special attention is given to interpretable artificial intelligence methods that allow not only making predictions but also explaining their causes.

Current achievements:

  • Developed a neural network model for classifying pathologies on X-ray images
  • Created an algorithm for time series analysis to monitor health indicators

Completed Projects:

Optimization of Big Data Processing Algorithms for Educational Platforms

2021-2023
University Internal Grant

This project was aimed at developing effective methods for analyzing educational data to improve the quality of learning. Within the project, algorithms for personalizing educational content and predicting academic performance were created.

The project results were implemented in the university's educational portal and allowed for an 18% increase in learning effectiveness according to the results of a pilot study.
